Hello! I hope you're having a great day! I’m a junior undergraduate at the University of Maryland studying bioinformatics in the College of Information Studies. As a research assistant at the Human-Computer Interaction Lab, I’ve loved exploring how people with early-onset dementia interface with online technologies and systems under Dr. Amanda Lazar and PhD candidate Emma Dixon. I’ve enjoyed continuing information behavior research as an Erevna Research Fellow, where I’m working to identify the core elements of self-care and how college students’ embrace of this health paradigm impacts their COVID-19 health outcomes. Previously, I’ve worked under Dr. Sarah Oates, a Senior Scholar at the University of Maryland College of Journalism, researching Russian propaganda through AI classifiers. I regularly code in Python, MATLAB, and R and am always trying to learn more about the intersections between public health and information technology.
